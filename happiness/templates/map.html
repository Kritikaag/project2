Map Charts
Select Year:

The world map visualization by year shows country-by-country the ‘happiness scores’ relative to GDP per capita. The ‘happiness scores’ are derived from self-reported statistics where people in over 150 countries rated their life satisfaction. People were asked to imagine themselves standing on a ladder, where the steps are numbered from 0 at the bottom to 10 at the top. The top of the ladder represents the best possible life for them, while the bottom represents the worst possible life. They were asked to rank where they personally stand on that ladder. There are significant differences across countries. From the map visualization, there appears to be a direct relationship between a country’s economic performance (gdp_per_capita) and the happiness of its citizens. The trend was tracked over a number of years from 2011 to 2020 to view consistency
