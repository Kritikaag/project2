World Happiness Report

About


In this project we explored The Happiness Index around the world.

As defined by the Happiness Council in 2012:

"The Happiness Index is defined as the weighted (by sampling weights) rate of respondents reporting “Very happy” or “Quite happy” less the weighted rate of respondents reporting “Not very happy” or “Not at all happy,” plus 100. The index thus ranges from 0 to 200"


There are 7 key metrics of measurement that together, make up the final Happiness Index. Thes 7 areas of measurement are:

GDP Per Capita   Social Support   Life Expectancy   Freedom   Generosity   Corruption   Happiness Rating

An overview of the 7 metrics and what they measure:

GDP Per Capita: Gross Domestic Product is the value of all the goods and services a country produces on a yearly basis

Social Support: “If you were in trouble, do you have relatives or friends you can count on to help you whenever you need them, or not?” (YES) 1 point (NO) 0 point

Life Expectancy: The estimate for the average number of “healthy” years a child at birth is estimated to live

Freedom: “Are you satisfied or dissatisfied with your freedom to choose what you do with your life?” (YES) 1 point (NO) 0 point

Generosity: Have you donated money to a charity in the past month?” (YES) 1 point (NO) 0 point

Corruption: “Is corruption widespread throughout the government or not?”   “Is corruption widespread within businesses or not?” Average of answers is recorded (YES) 1 point (NO) 0 point

Happiness Rating: Refers to how much of the Happiness Index cannot be explained by social measures and the other metrics.


Where questions are asked, these are answered by applicants in the Gallup World Poll, where The Happiness Report sources their data.
